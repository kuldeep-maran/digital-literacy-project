## Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. This is usually done through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted sources.

A common scenario involves a student receiving an email that looks like it is from a bank or a popular service. The email may claim that the account will be blocked unless the user verifies their details. It contains a link that redirects to a fake website that looks very similar to the original one.

When the user enters their login details, the attacker captures this information and uses it to access the real account. This can lead to financial loss, data theft, or identity misuse.

Students and young users are often targeted because they may not always verify links carefully. The consequences can be serious, including loss of money and privacy.

Therefore, it is important to stay alert and verify all online communications before sharing any personal information.
